Beginner Yoga Routine

Practice these poses to improve flexibility, strength, and relaxation.

1. Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

How to do it: Stand tall with feet together, arms at sides. Distribute weight evenly, engage thighs, lift chest, and reach arms overhead with palms facing each other.

Benefits: Improves posture and balance; serves as a foundational pose for other asanas.

2. Child’s Pose (Balasana)

How to do it: Kneel on the floor, touch big toes together, and sit back on heels. Separate knees hip-width apart, exhale, and lay torso down between thighs. Extend arms forward, palms down.

Benefits: Gently stretches the hips, thighs, and ankles; calms the brain and relieves stress.

3. Cat-Cow Pose (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

How to do it: Begin on hands and knees in a tabletop position. Inhale, arch back, and lift head and tailbone (Cow Pose). Exhale, round spine, tuck chin to chest (Cat Pose). Repeat for several breaths.

Benefits: Increases flexibility of the spine; stretches the back and neck.

4. Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

How to do it: From tabletop position, tuck toes under and lift hips toward the ceiling, forming an inverted V shape. Keep hands shoulder-width apart and feet hip-width apart.

Benefits: Strengthens the arms and legs; stretches the shoulders, hamstrings, calves, arches, and hands.

5. Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

How to do it: Step right foot forward into a lunge position, keeping left foot turned slightly inward. Raise arms overhead, palms facing each other. Bend right knee directly over right ankle.

Benefits: Strengthens the legs, opens the hips and chest, and stretches the arms and legs.

6. Tree Pose (Vrikshasana)

How to do it: Stand tall, shift weight onto left foot, and place sole of right foot on inner left thigh or calf (avoid the knee). Bring hands together in a prayer position at chest or raise them overhead.

Benefits: Improves balance and stability in the legs; strengthens the thighs, calves, ankles, and spine.

7. Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

How to do it: Lie face down on the floor with legs extended and tops of feet flat. Place hands under shoulders. Inhale, press into hands, and lift chest off the floor, keeping elbows slightly bent.

Benefits: Strengthens the spine; stretches the chest, lungs, shoulders, and abdomen.

8. Corpse Pose (Savasana)

How to do it: Lie flat on your back with legs extended and arms at sides, palms facing up. Close eyes and breathe naturally.

Benefits: Promotes relaxation; reduces stress and anxiety.
