Simple Home Workouts

Bodyweight workouts for all levels — no equipment needed!

Exercises

TRY IT YOURSELF

Beginner Exercises

Jumping Jacks

  1. Stand tall with your arms at your sides and feet together.
  2. Jump your feet out to the sides while simultaneously raising your arms overhead.
  3. Immediately reverse the movement by jumping your feet back together and lowering your arms to your sides.
  4. Repeat rapidly for 30 seconds.

Wall Sit

  1. Stand with your back against a flat wall.
  2. Slide down the wall by bending your knees until your thighs are parallel to the ground, forming a 90-degree angle at your hips and knees.
  3. Ensure your back remains flat against the wall throughout the exercise.
  4. Hold this position for 30–60 seconds.
  5. Slowly push back up to the starting position.

Push-Ups (on knees)

  1. Start on your hands and knees, with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, directly under your shoulders.
  2. Lower your chest towards the floor by bending your elbows, keeping your body in a straight line from your head to your knees.
  3. Continue lowering until your chest is just above the ground.
  4. Push back up to the starting position by straightening your arms.
  5. Repeat for 10–15 repetitions.

Bodyweight Squats

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing slightly outward.
  2. Keep your chest up and your core engaged.
  3. Lower your hips back and down as if you were sitting in a chair, keeping your back straight.
  4. Continue lowering until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as low as is comfortable.
  5. Push through your heels to return to the starting standing position.
  6. Repeat for 15–20 repetitions.

Step-Ups (on stairs)

  1. Stand facing a sturdy step or chair.
  2. Place your right foot firmly on the step.
  3. Push through your right heel to lift your body up onto the step, bringing your left foot to meet your right foot on top.
  4. Step down with your left foot first, followed by your right foot.
  5. Repeat, alternating leading legs for 10 repetitions per leg.

Intermediate Exercises

Plank to Push-Up

  1. Start in a forearm plank position with your elbows directly under your shoulders and your body in a straight line.
  2. Place your right hand on the ground directly under your right shoulder, then push up onto your right hand.
  3. Follow with your left hand, pushing up into a standard push-up position.
  4. Lower back down to your forearms one arm at a time, returning to the plank position.
  5. Repeat the sequence, alternating which hand leads the movement for 30 seconds.

Tricep Dips (on a chair)

  1. Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair with your hands placed shoulder-width apart, gripping the edge of the seat.
  2. Extend your legs out in front of you.
  3. Slide your hips off the front of the chair, supporting your weight with your hands.
  4. Lower your body by bending your elbows, keeping your back close to the chair.
  5. Continue lowering until your elbows are at about a 90-degree angle.
  6. Push back up to the starting position by straightening your arms.
  7. Repeat for a desired number of repetitions.

Mountain Climbers

  1. Start in a high plank position with your hands directly under your shoulders and your body in a straight line.
  2. Bring your right knee towards your chest, keeping your foot off the ground.
  3. Quickly switch legs, bringing your left knee towards your chest while extending your right leg back.
  4. Continue alternating legs in a running motion, maintaining a stable core and straight body line for 30–45 seconds.

Jump Squats

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, chest up, and core engaged.
  2. Lower into a squat position, keeping your back straight and thighs parallel to the ground.
  3. From the bottom of the squat, explosively jump upwards, extending your legs fully in the air.
  4. Land softly back into the squat position, ready for the next jump.
  5. Repeat for 12–15 repetitions.

Side Lunges

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart.
  2. Step your right leg out to the side, keeping your left leg straight.
  3. Bend your right knee and lower your hips as if you were sitting down, keeping your right foot flat on the ground.
  4. Ensure your left leg remains straight, but don't lock your knee.
  5. Push off with your right foot to return to the starting position.
  6. Repeat on the left side by stepping your left leg out.
  7. Continue alternating sides for a desired number of repetitions.

Advanced Exercises

Burpees

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and arms at your sides.
  2. Drop into a squat position with your hands flat on the floor in front of you.
  3. Kick your feet back, landing in a push-up position.
  4. Perform one push-up (optional, but recommended for a full burpee).
  5. Jump your feet back up to your hands, returning to the squat position.
  6. Explosively jump up into the air, reaching your arms overhead.
  7. Land softly and immediately go into the next repetition.
  8. Repeat for 30–60 seconds.

Pistol Squats

  1. Stand on one leg, extending the other leg straight out in front of you. You can hold your arms out for balance.
  2. Slowly lower your body into a deep squat on your standing leg, keeping your extended leg off the ground. Try to bring your hips as close to your heel as possible.
  3. Maintain control throughout the movement and keep your chest up.
  4. Push through the heel of your standing leg to return to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for 6–8 repetitions on one leg, then switch to the other leg.

Handstand Hold

  1. Start facing a wall. Place your hands shoulder-width apart on the floor, about a foot away from the wall.
  2. Kick up one leg at a time against the wall, bringing your body into an inverted vertical position (handstand).
  3. Once stable, engage your core muscles and maintain a straight line from your wrists to your heels.
  4. Hold this position for 20–60 seconds, breathing steadily.
  5. Carefully come down by lowering one leg at a time.

One-Arm Push-Up

  1. Start in a standard push-up position with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart for added stability.
  2. Place one hand behind your back.
  3. Lower your chest towards the floor by bending your supporting arm's elbow, keeping your body in a straight line. Lower down as far as you can control.
  4. Push back up to the starting position using the strength of your supporting arm.
  5. Repeat on the other arm.
